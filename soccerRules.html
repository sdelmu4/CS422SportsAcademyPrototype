About Soccer

Soccer, also known as football in other parts of the world, is a sport where two teams of 11 players each compete against one another to end the game with the most goals. Among the 11 players on a team, there is a goalkeeper who's the primary defender for your team. Each match at a professional level lasts 90 minutes. This is divided with two 45-minute halves and after the first half, a 10-15 minute break is given.

Games are played on a pitch that typically measure 120 by 75 meters. The ball that is used is created from synthetic materials to make sure that it can withstand all environment and weather conditions. Lastly, there is a team uniform that is followed for each team. This usually consists of jerseys (goalie having a seperate uniform than the rest), shorts, socks, shin guards for protection, and cleats!

Do's and Don'ts

Below talks about what is and isn't allowed during a soccer match:

Allowed:

  • Control and move the ball using your feet or Head
  • Committing tackles that win the ball and are not purposed around injuring or endangering your opponent
  • As a goalkeeper, you're allowed to use your hands to pick up the ball and handle it only within your penalty area.
  • Playing the game tactically is allowed and encouraged. This means you're not punished for passing the ball back towards your own half, shielding the ball so that the opponent doesn't get to it, or even committing tactical fouls that are within reason.

Not Allowed:

  • Using the ball with your hands as an outfield player
  • Receiving the ball while in an offside position. You are considered to be offside whenever the ball is received and your head, body, or feet are nearer to the opponents goal line than both the ball and the second to last opponent.
  • Overly committed physical contact and excessive force such as pushing, tripping, and holding is not allowed.
  • Unsportsmanship such as delaying to play the ball back in game, misconduct towards players, referees, or staff, or inappropriate behavior is not allowed and can even lead to expelling the player.

Yellow Cards And Red Cards

Based on how severe a foul is committed, a player may receieve a yellow or red card. These cards represent the "status" of a player and whether they're in good standing if they have no cards, are given warnings if given a yellow card, or if ejected from the game if given a red card. Note that, if given two yellow cards in the same game, it'll convert your cards into a red, ejecting you from the game as well. This leaves your team down one player that cannot be brought back, meaning your total number of players goes from 11 to 10.
