Soccer Skills

Skills are used to get an advantage against your opponent and create an opening to pass the ball, analyze the pitch to plan your team's next move, or creating the chance that allows you to shoot and score. Skills are an essential to the sport, and can change the flow and dynamic of the game when properly executed. It's crucial to understand what skills can help you in your game whenever you step foot on the pitch, and this applies no matter the position you decide to play.

Important Skills

Below are a list of skills that anyone can benefit from:

  • Dribbling: Being able to move the ball while keeping control of the ball. This is highly effective is able to perform with speed and composure.
  • Passing: Being able to perform different types of passes can elevate your game and help out your team. Performing accurate short and long passes are key, as well as being able to execute through passes that send the ball behind defenders for your teammates to run onto.
  • Set Pieces: Performing accurate crosses from corners or free kicks can give your team the advantage whenever a foul is called outside of your opponents box.
  • Game awareness: Believe it or not, a simple thing such as awareness is not an easy thing to practice. Knowing where every player is on the pitch, where your teammates position is at to attack, and being able to make "right" decisions at a high level is a key trait every player must strive for.

How to Improve your Skills

Here is a list of ways that should be practiced to enhance your skills:

  • Practicing drills are a great way to train your mind and body into being able to perform these skills. Using cones to dribble around, targets to shoot at, and performing passes against a wall or with someone else will definitely help.
  • Spectating others play, especially those whose playstyle you want to adapt to, can also be beneficial. Sometimes it can take watching someone else play to be able to take in and learn how and why they're performing a skill.
  • Playing in smaller games with a smaller pitch than usual can improve how you react in certain situations and help you develop your decision making. Because of the tighter space available, you'll be forced to learn and adapt to quick time actions.
  • Making sure your strength and conditioning is at its best is also key. Not only can it help fight off injuries, but it also plays a major role in how long you can perform at your best until you start to feel fatigue.
