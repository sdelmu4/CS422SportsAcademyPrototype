Soccer Injuries

Injuries in soccer are relatively common because of how intense and physical games can get. They range anywhere from minor bruises to major career-changing experiences that could potentially leave a player medically unclear to never step on the pitch again. Injuries are caused by tackles, collisions, fouls, but also happen due to sudden movements, poor playing conditions, and fatigue.

With proper training, quality equipment and fields to play on, a lot of injuries can be prevented. Diet, decision making, and proper warm-ups/stretches before a game also contribute to how severe an injury can be.

Common Injuries

Below are a list of the most common injuries that occour:

  • Sprains and Strains: Happens when changing direction too fast while sprinting, or by overstretching your muscles while sprinting.
  • Knee Injuries: Tearing your ACL (anterior cruciate ligament) or doing too many twisting/squatting movements frequently.
  • Head and Neck Injuries: Occours during hard landings when jumping for the ball or from head collisions against another player.
  • Upper Body Injuries: Sprains or dislocating your shoulder from jumping for the ball and landing wrong.

Injury Statistics

Below are some statistics regarding injuries in soccer:

  • At a professional level, on average players face about 6-9 injuries per 1000 hours of match play, or about 66 full matches played.
  • Muscle strains and ligament sprains make up approximately 65% of all soccer injuries, with hamstring injuries being the most common (making up about 16% of total injuries).
  • While they are rare, concussions are recorded to occour about once per 1000 hours of match play, or about 66 full matches played.
  • Majority of injuries occour in the legs (85%), with upper body injuries being the second most frequent injuries (10%) and injuries to the head and neck being last (5%).
